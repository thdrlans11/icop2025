Submission Guidelines

  • Abstracts should be written in English and submitted trough the conference website.
  • Select your preferred type for your presentation. The Scientific Committee reserves the right to make the final decision on the mode of presentation.
  • Abstracts should be written in English and submitted trough the conference website.
  • The Presenting Author of the abstract should be one of the authors listed in the abstract.
  • Name and Affiliation of Co-author(s) and confirmation that all authors have consented to the submission.
  • The submitter should be the first author of the paper.
  • The Presenting Author must register for the conference.
※ Notice of Encore Abstract ※
  • The presented abstract can be presented but it should be contained an updated date. (The Scientific Committee will be judged.)
  • The abstract should be presented as an abstract and it has been not acceptable published as a paper.
  • The Encore abstract should be marked on the abstract or presentation file (such as Poster or Oral Presentation Slide).
  • The presenter should be guaranteed that there are no copyright issues with the previous presentation.

Abstract Format

  • Please adhere to the following formatting guidelines when submitting abstracts for the conference.
Abstract Format
  • 1)
    Title:
    • The Title should not exceed 20 words (First letter of each word in uppercase, except for articles, prepositions, and conjunctions).
    • Capitalize the first letter of the sentence only.
  • 2)
    Authors:
    • Name of Presentation Author who will act as the main contact for this abstract.
    • Do not include degrees or titles.
  • 3)
    Affiliations:
    • Each author should be listed by department, institution, city and country.
  • 4)
    Body of Abstract:
    • Word Limit: 300 words (excluding the Title, Authors, and Affiliations).
    • The abstract should be structured chronologically into 4 sections
    • An Acceptable Structure of an abstract must consist of the following components.
    Background/Aims A statement of the hypothesis or research question.
    Methods An explanation of the study design and experimental methods used.
    Results A concise summary of the major findings of the experiment or study.
    Sufficient data must be provided to permit evaluation by the reviewers and public reading the abstract.
    Statements such as “The date will be discussed” are not acceptable.
    Conclusions Summary of the overall findings and the importance of the study
